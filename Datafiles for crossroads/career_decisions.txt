CAREER & STARTUP DECISIONS - Research & Statistics

CAREER CHANGE STATISTICS
- 52% of workers are considering changing jobs in 2024 (Microsoft Work Trend Index)
- Average person changes careers 5-7 times in their lifetime (BLS)
- 80% of people who make a major career change report higher job satisfaction after 2 years
- Career changers who plan for 6+ months are 3x more likely to succeed than impulsive switchers
- The average career transition takes 11 months from decision to new role

STARTUP & ENTREPRENEURSHIP
- 90% of startups fail within 10 years (Investopedia)
- 21.5% of startups fail in year 1, 30% in year 2, 50% by year 5 (Bureau of Labor Statistics)
- Founders who have failed before have a 20% success rate vs 18% for first-timers
- Average startup founder age at founding: 45 years old (MIT Sloan study)
- Startups with 2 co-founders raise 30% more money and grow 3x faster than solo founders
- Median runway before profitability: 2-3 years
- 82% of businesses that fail do so because of cash flow problems

STAYING VS LEAVING
- Employees who stay at a company for 2+ years earn 50% less over their lifetime than those who move
- Internal promotions take on average 3 years; external hires get 18-20% salary premium
- Job satisfaction peaks at 2 years and 7 years at a company
- "Quiet quitting" costs US economy $500B annually in lost productivity

FINANCIAL RUNWAY FOR CAREER CHANGES
- Financial advisors recommend 6-12 months of savings before leaving stable employment
- Founders typically need $50,000-$150,000 personal runway to survive first year
- 65% of founders use personal savings as primary startup funding

PSYCHOLOGICAL FACTORS
- Regret from inaction ("I wish I had tried") outlasts regret from action ("I wish I hadn't") by 2:1 ratio
- Decision fatigue peaks at 3-6 months of deliberation — extended deliberation rarely improves outcomes
- People overestimate how bad failure will feel and underestimate their resilience
