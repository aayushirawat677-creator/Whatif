DECISION MAKING PSYCHOLOGY - Research & Statistics

COGNITIVE SCIENCE OF DECISIONS
- Humans make approximately 35,000 decisions per day
- Decision fatigue reduces decision quality by up to 40% (Roy Baumeister)
- The "paradox of choice" — more options correlate with lower satisfaction (Barry Schwartz)
- Optimal number of options for highest satisfaction: 3-5 choices
- Intuitive decisions are as accurate as analytical decisions for complex personal choices

REGRET RESEARCH
- Action regret fades faster than inaction regret over time
- At end of life, people regret what they didn't do 2x more than what they did
- "Anticipated regret" is a useful decision tool — imagine how you'll feel in 10 years
- Jeff Bezos "Regret Minimization Framework" — project yourself to age 80

RISK PERCEPTION
- People overestimate the probability of negative outcomes by average 2x
- "Negativity bias" — bad outcomes feel 2-2.5x more impactful than equivalent good ones
- Fear of failure is the #1 blocker to career and life decisions (Harvard Business Review)
- Most feared outcomes (financial ruin, social rejection) are far less permanent than imagined

FRAMEWORKS FOR BETTER DECISIONS
- Pre-mortem analysis: imagine the decision failed, work backwards to understand why
- "10-10-10" rule: how will this feel in 10 minutes, 10 months, 10 years?
- Seek "good enough" not "perfect" — satisficing outperforms maximizing for life decisions
- Consult 3 people who have faced similar decisions, not people who will validate you

TIMING OF DECISIONS
- Morning decisions are higher quality than evening decisions
- Major life decisions made during periods of stress have 35% lower satisfaction rate
- "Sleep on it" is scientifically validated — unconscious processing improves complex decisions
- Decision quality improves significantly when basic needs (sleep, food, exercise) are met

SOCIAL INFLUENCE ON DECISIONS
- People are 3x more likely to make a major life change when someone in their social circle does
- Social comparison drives 60% of major life decisions
- Consulting too many people ("decision by committee") reduces satisfaction with outcomes
- The person whose opinion matters most is your future self, not your current social circle
